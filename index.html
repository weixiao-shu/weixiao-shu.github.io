Wei Xiao, Ph.D. in Engineering, is a professor and doctoral supervisor at the School of Computer Engineering and Science, Shanghai University. From 1993 to 1997, he studied at Shandong University and obtained a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Applications. From 2009 to 2014, he studied at Shanghai University and earned a Ph.D. in Computer Application Technology. From 2012 to 2013, he conducted visiting research in the Department of Computer Science at City University of Hong Kong. From 2015 to 2017, he pursued postdoctoral research at the Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences. His main research directions include natural language processing, cross-modal semantic analysis, machine learning, knowledge graphs, and more. He has led 5 national-level research projects, 4 provincial/ministerial-level research projects, and 2 sub-projects under the Key R&D Program of the Ministry of Science and Technology. As a key contributor, he has participated in over 10 projects, including key projects of the National Natural Science Foundation of China, major projects of the National 863 Program, and provincial/ministerial-level research projects. He has published more than 50 SCI/EI-indexed papers in top international journals and conferences, with a total citation count of over 1,000, including authoritative journals and conferences in the field of artificial intelligence such as IEEE TFS, TLT, TII, THMS, FGCS, ACL, and EMNLP. His research outcomes have been applied in legal intelligence, government intelligence, medical intelligence, and other domains, and he has led over 20 software development projects.
